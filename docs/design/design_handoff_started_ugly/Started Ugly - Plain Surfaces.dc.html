Airbnb

Est. 2008

Three guys who couldn't make rent put air mattresses on their floor — and a craigslist clone online to fill them.

Drag the handle — Airbnb, 2008 → 2026.

The ugly part. A one-page site cloned from craigslist. Grey placeholder photos, no map, no payments, no reviews. To book, you emailed the host and hoped they were real. The founders funded it selling novelty cereal door to door.

What they shipped anyway. They charged real money before any of it worked. Three guests, two weeks of rent covered — enough signal to keep building the parts they'd faked.

Now. A public company worth roughly $90B, hosting more than 5 million stays a night across 220+ countries.

About
Function over form — a directory of embarrassing first versions

You only ever see the after.

Every product you admire showed up in your life fully formed — polished, confident, obviously inevitable. So that's the bar you hold your own draft to. It's a rigged comparison. You're putting your day one next to their year fifteen.

Started Ugly collects the day ones. The lesson underneath all of them is the same three words: function over form. Make it work before you make it pretty. Ship the part that's useful and be embarrassed about the rest.

"If you aren't embarrassed by the first version of your product, you've shipped too late."— Reid Hoffman
What we believe
  1. Shipping ugly beats polishing in private. Every time.
  2. The embarrassing version is the one that proves it's real.
  3. Survivorship bias hides the ugly start, not the absence of one.
  4. Done and embarrassing > perfect and imaginary.

This site was built in a weekend and it shows. Default fonts, hairline rules, no logo yet. No focus group approved any of it.

That's the point.
