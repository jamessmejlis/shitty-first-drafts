A one-page site cloned from craigslist. Grey placeholder photos, no map, no payments, no reviews. To book, you emailed the host and hoped they were real. The founders funded it selling novelty cereal door to door.

WHAT THEY SHIPPED ANYWAY

They charged real money before any of it worked. Three guests, two weeks of rent covered — enough signal to keep building the parts they'd faked.

NOW

/ ABOUT
FUNCTION OVER FORM · A DIRECTORY OF EMBARRASSING FIRST VERSIONS

You only ever see the
after.

Every product you admire showed up in your life fully formed — polished, confident, obviously inevitable. So that's the bar you hold your own draft to. It's a rigged comparison. You're putting your day one next to their year fifteen.

Started Ugly collects the day ones. The craigslist clones, the duct-taped demos, the things that didn't scale and the founders who charged money before any of it worked. The lesson underneath all of them is the same three words: function over form. Make it work before you make it pretty. Ship the part that's useful and be embarrassed about the rest.

"If you aren't embarrassed by the first version of your product, you've shipped too late."— REID HOFFMAN
WHAT WE BELIEVE
01
Shipping ugly beats polishing in private. Every time.
02
The embarrassing version is the one that proves it's real.
03
Survivorship bias hides the ugly start, not the absence of one.
04
Done and embarrassing > perfect and imaginary.

This site was built in a weekend and it shows. The type is a little tight, the jokes are a little dry, and no focus group approved any of it.

That's the point.
